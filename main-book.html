What I Did and Why I Did It

In our engineering project, I contributed significantly to various aspects across Labs 1–5, each building on the overarching goal of designing an efficient and sustainable irrigation system for Canadian corn farming by creating a concrete half-pipe for our solution. My involvement ranged from foundational analysis to hands-on prototyping, decision-making, and critical assessments. My roles were selected based on their alignment with my skills in manufacturing processes, problem-solving, and adaptability to dynamic challenges.

How I Achieved It

Lab 1: I focused on defining the project's Functions, Objectives, and Constraints (FOC). This step was crucial for establishing clear, measurable parameters to guide the design process​.
Lab 2: I created the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) for Lab 1 and contributed to stakeholder analysis, including a stakeholder landscape with detailed categorizations and rationales. This systematic approach ensured comprehensive planning and alignment with stakeholder needs.
Lab 3: I evaluated the quality and creativity of novel solutions like the Improved Rolling Sprinklers and Concrete Half-Pipes. This ideation phase involved shortlisting practical solutions and refining our design criteria based on creativity and feasibility​.
Lab 4: I managed the entire manufacturing process, from prototyping the Concrete Half-Pipe system to dealing with team dynamics and meeting tight deadlines under pressure. Additionally, I worked to mitigate unexpected challenges such as resource limitations and design flaws​.
Lab 5: I led the Risk Assessment process, where I identified risks across design, management, and sustainability categories. Using a color-coded matrix, I prioritized and proposed mitigation strategies for high-impact risks

Reflection

This project challenged me to balance creativity with practicality, especially under constraints like budget and environmental impact. One critical takeaway was learning how to adapt to evolving circumstances—whether addressing unexpected delays or resolving design inefficiencies during prototyping. Moreover, working on the risk assessment deepened my understanding of anticipating challenges and strategizing effective solutions.

Outcome

By integrating technical expertise with structured planning and teamwork, our project successfully addressed water conservation in corn farming, offering an innovative solution that holds promise for sustainable agriculture.
